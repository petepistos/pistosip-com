Training library

45 modules. Three depth levels. One documented program.

Every module covers a core security domain at three levels of depth — introductory for general staff, intermediate for those with system access, and advanced for technical and leadership roles.

Program design

Built around what examiners look for.

Every element of the Mathisi program is mapped to the specific training requirements in the regulations your organization faces — not a generic security awareness catalog.

Current threat content

Training content is updated to reflect current attack vectors sourced from CISA advisories and FBI IC3 threat feeds. Staff are trained on the threats active in your industry today, not last year's examples.

Role-based delivery

Introductory, intermediate, and advanced tracks ensure that training is appropriate to each staff member's role and system access level — satisfying role-based training requirements in CMMC and HIPAA.

Dated completion records

Every training completion is recorded with a timestamp, employee name, module, and score. When an examiner asks for training documentation, the record is already organized and ready.

Sentinel integration

Training results post directly to the client's Sentinel compliance record — linked to the CIS controls and regulatory requirements they satisfy. No manual evidence entry required.

Remedial assignment

Staff who do not meet the required score are automatically assigned remedial training. The remediation is tracked and documented — closing the loop on the evidence record.

Annual program cycle

Training campaigns are scheduled and managed on an annual cycle aligned to each client's regulatory calendar — NY DFS requires annual training, as does HIPAA, GLBA, and CMMC.

Regulatory framework mapping

Each module is tagged to the regulatory requirements it satisfies. A training completion record shows not just what was completed, but which NY DFS, HIPAA, or CMMC obligation it addresses.

Board-level reporting

Aggregate training completion rates, module scores, and program status are available in Sentinel's board reporting output — suitable for submission to regulators and board review.

New hire onboarding

New staff are enrolled in the introductory track automatically. CMMC requires training before system access is granted — Mathisi creates the documented record that satisfies this requirement.

How results become evidence

Training completions feed Sentinel automatically.

Training completion records are not stored in a separate system. They land directly in the client's Sentinel record — linked to the CIS controls they satisfy and the regulatory requirements they address.

01

Campaign runs

Training campaign deployed to target population. Completion, scores, and time-on-module all logged with timestamps per employee.

02

Results captured

Pass and fail rates recorded per employee. Remedial training automatically assigned to those who did not meet the threshold within 10 business days.

03

Sentinel updated

Results post to the client's Sentinel record. CIS 14.1, 14.2, and 14.5 evidence status updates automatically. Compliance score recalculates.

04

Examiner-ready record

Dated completion record, participant list, scores, and remediation documentation — organized and ready when the examiner asks for training evidence.
