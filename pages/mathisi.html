Aegis is a virtual CISO engagement — a seasoned security leader takes the helm of your cybersecurity program without the cost of a full-time hire. The vCISO sets strategy, carries weight in examiner and carrier conversations, and directs three coordinated service pillars: Mathisi, Sentinel, and Skopein.

Mathisi is the pillar that reaches every employee. It moves the security program out of the filing cabinet and into the workday — through tiered training, monthly refreshers, realistic phishing simulations, and debriefs of real breaches relevant to your industry.

The aim is not to satisfy a training checkbox. The aim is that your workforce becomes the first line of defense — informed, practiced, and accountable.

Introduction to Cybersecurity
Foundational training every employee takes at onboarding — the baseline vocabulary, the everyday threats, and the behaviors that prevent most incidents before they begin.
Intermediate
Role-specific training for staff with elevated access — producers, account managers, finance, HR — tuned to the attacks that target their specific workflows.
Advanced
Deep training for IT leadership, privileged users, and designated incident handlers — how to recognize a live incident and how to act in the first fifteen minutes.
Training & Awareness
Monthly refreshers and annual workforce certifications — with dated completion records linked to each employee, ready for an examiner or underwriter to review.
Phishing Simulations
Realistic phishing campaigns targeted to your industry and workflows, with after-click coaching for anyone who engages — no shame, no spectacle, just repetition.
Real Security Events
Debriefs of actual breaches at peer organizations — what happened, how it was noticed, what it cost, and what would have changed the outcome. Generic content doesn't move the needle; neighbors' stories do.

How Mathisi fits the program

Mathisi is the most visible pillar to your staff because it's the one they consume. But its value to the compliance program is less about what people watch and more about what it produces: dated training completion records, phishing simulation results linked to each employee, and the workforce competency documentation every major framework requires.

Those records feed directly into Sentinel's evidence base. When Sentinel surfaces a finding from Skopein — a misconfigured account, a stale permission, a repeated click on a phishing test — Mathisi is where the behavioral remediation happens. The three pillars close the loop: scan, document, train.
